# Project Overview – Research Lab Manager

## Purpose of the System

The **Research Lab Manager** system is designed to support and streamline
operations within a university research laboratory. The goal is to replace
scattered spreadsheets and ad‑hoc tracking methods with a **centralized
database system** that manages the full research lifecycle.

The system enables lab administrators to:
- Track lab members and their roles
- Manage research projects and their funding
- Monitor equipment and device usage
- Record publications and authorship
- Enforce mentorship rules and reporting constraints

The database supports querying, updating, and reporting to assist in lab
administration and research oversight.

---

## Core Entities in the Mini‑World

Based on the analysis of the project requirements, the following **core
entities** exist in the system:

### 1. Lab Members
Represents all individuals associated with the research lab.

**Common attributes:**
- Member ID
- Name
- Join date

**Specialized roles (disjoint):**
- **Faculty**: Includes department information and may lead projects
- **Students**: Includes student number, academic level, and major
- **External Collaborators**: Includes institutional affiliation and CV

Each lab member must participate in **at least one project**.

---

### 2. Research Projects
Represents research initiatives conducted within the lab.

**Key attributes:**
- Project ID
- Title
- Start date and end date
- Planned duration
- Status (active, completed, paused)

Each project:
- Is led by exactly **one faculty member**
- May or may not be funded by a grant
- Has multiple lab members working on it, with defined roles and weekly hours

---

### 3. Grants
Represents financial funding provided to support projects.

**Key attributes:**
- Grant ID
- Funding agency
- Budget
- Start date
- Expected duration

Each grant funds **one project**, while a project may have **zero or more grants**.

---

### 4. Equipment and Devices
Represents laboratory resources used by members during research activities.

- **Equipment** represents a general equipment type or model
- **Devices** represent the physical units of that equipment

**Device attributes:**
- Device number (unique within an equipment type)
- Purchase date
- Status (available, in use, retired)

---

### 5. Equipment Usage
Captures how lab members use devices.

**Key attributes:**
- Usage start date
- Usage end date
- Purpose of use

This entity connects **Lab Members** and **Devices**.

---

### 6. Publications
Represents research output generated by lab members.

**Key attributes:**
- Publication ID
- Title
- Publication month and year
- Venue
- Optional DOI

Each publication:
- Must be authored by **at least one lab member**
- Typically involves multiple authors

---

### 7. Mentorship
Represents mentorship relationships within the lab.

**Key attributes:**
- Mentor
- Mentee
- Start date
- End date

Mentorship is modeled as a **recursive relationship** between lab members.

---

## Real‑World Constraints and Business Rules

Several important real‑world constraints govern the behavior of the system:

### Mentorship Constraints
- A mentee may have **at most one mentor**
- A lab member cannot mentor themselves
- **Students cannot mentor faculty members**

---

### Equipment Usage Constraints
- A device may be used by **no more than three lab members simultaneously**
- Device availability depends on its current status

---

### Project and Participation Constraints
- Every lab member must work on **at least one project**
- Each project has **exactly one faculty leader**
- Weekly hours and roles are recorded for each member’s participation

---

### Publication Constraints
- Every publication must have **one or more authors**
- Publication dates are recorded at the **month and year** level

---

## Summary

The Research Lab Manager mini‑world models a realistic academic research
environment that integrates people, projects, resources, funding, and
research output. While some constraints cannot be fully enforced at the
conceptual level, the database design captures all required data and
supports enforcement through relational constraints, SQL, and application
logic in later phases of the project.
